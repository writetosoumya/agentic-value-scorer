CORE PREMISE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ROI is not a complete value framework. It is a savings calculator with a blind spot the size of an AI portfolio.

Existing frameworks measure financial return or governance risk. None combines both into a single adjusted score with strategic optionality as an explicit dimension. That is the gap AVRE™ fills.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
THE AVRE™ FORMULA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

NEV (Net Enterprise Value) = (ROI + ROE + ROF) − Total Cost of Agentification − ARM™ Risk Penalty

The formula produces a single governed score between 0 and 100 that reflects financial return, operational quality, strategic optionality, implementation cost, and risk exposure simultaneously.

THE THREE LENSES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ROI — RETURN ON INVESTMENT (Financial Lens)
The financial lens. Cost saved. Revenue lifted. Hours recovered.
The lens everyone uses. Never enough on its own.
Measures: direct cost reduction, productivity gains, revenue impact, time-to-value.
Limitation: captures volume improvement but not quality, sustainability, or risk.

ROE — RETURN ON EFFORT (Operational Lens)
The operational lens. Did output quality improve? Did rework drop? Did people get to do better work or just more of it?
The lens most platforms ignore.
Measures: output quality delta, error rate change, rework reduction, employee experience impact.
Key question: Did agentification improve the quality of work, or just the quantity?

ROF — RETURN ON FUTURE (Strategic Lens)
The strategic lens. Did this build a reusable capability — or a dead end?
The lens that justifies AI to a CEO, not just a CFO.
Measures: reusable capability created, platform effect (does this enable future deployments?), competitive optionality, institutional knowledge preservation vs. transfer.
Key question: If you removed this agent tomorrow, what breaks — and what remains?

WHY ARM™ RISK PENALTY CHANGES EVERYTHING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Example: A clinical triage agent with 300% ROI. Zero governance architecture. Critical ARM™ flags across compliance, bias, and transparency. NEV score: 28.

The ROI said scale it. The NEV said it is a liability waiting for a headline.

That is the conversation AVRE™ forces before the headline happens.

High ROI with high ARM™ risk penalty produces a low or negative NEV. This is the core governance insight: raw financial return is not the same as enterprise value when risk is properly accounted for.

PRE-REVIEW DIAGNOSTIC — FOUR QUESTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Run these before any AI investment review:

1. Has output quality actually improved since launch, or just volume?
2. What reusable capability did this create for future initiatives?
3. Is the ARM™ risk profile documented, monitored, and owned?
4. If you removed this agent tomorrow, what breaks?

If any answer is "I don't know" — that is not a measurement problem. It is a governance architecture problem.

AVRE™ IN THE BROADER FRAMEWORK ARCHITECTURE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

AVRE™ is the third layer in a three-framework governance architecture:

Are we building the right AI?        → TraceSense™
Are we building it responsibly?       → ARM™
Are we creating real enterprise value? → AVRE™

Value and risk are necessary. Not sufficient. Enterprise AI also needs regulatory clearance, lifecycle cost intelligence, and a human decision hierarchy.

The vision: one platform synthesizes all layers into a single go/no-go signal before an ideation meeting.

SCORING CALIBRATION NOTES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ROI Score calibration:
- Score reflects financial return relative to total cost of agentification
- Must account for implementation cost, operational cost, and change management cost
- High volume savings with poor quality outcomes should NOT produce a high ROI score

ROE Score calibration:
- Not just efficiency — measures whether human effort was redirected to higher-value work
- A use case that saves hours but increases rework or degrades output quality scores LOW on ROE
- Employee experience and capability development are valid ROE signals

ROF Score calibration:
- Highest scores go to use cases that create reusable platform capabilities
- Single-use automations with no architectural carry-forward score LOW on ROF
- Reusable agent frameworks, shared governance infrastructure, and capability-building deployments score HIGH

ARM™ Risk Penalty application:
- The risk penalty is not additive — it is multiplicative in effect
- A deployment with critical ARM™ flags (score > 7.5 composite) should produce a NEV score in the 0–39 range regardless of ROI
- Risk penalty increases nonlinearly with ARM™ composite score
