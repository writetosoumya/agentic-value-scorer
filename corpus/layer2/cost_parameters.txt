SECTION 2: AI IMPLEMENTATION COST BENCHMARKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

These benchmarks represent Meridian's historical and industry-benchmarked costs for AI implementation by complexity tier. Use these as baseline estimates in business cases. Actual costs require formal project scoping.

TIER 1 — SIMPLE AI DEPLOYMENT
Definition: Pre-built AI tool or API integration with minimal customization. Limited data pipeline work. Single use case, single team.
Examples: Internal chatbot using off-the-shelf LLM, document summarization tool, basic classification model using existing data.
Typical cost range: $85,000 — $250,000
Typical timeline: 6 — 16 weeks to production
Typical team: 2-3 engineers, 1 product manager, 0.5 data engineer
Key cost drivers: Integration effort, data preparation, user training, change management

TIER 2 — MODERATE AI DEPLOYMENT
Definition: Custom model development or significant LLM customization. Multiple data sources. Moderate integration complexity. RAG systems, fine-tuned models, agentic tools with defined scope.
Examples: RAG-powered knowledge management system, claims triage agent, underwriting support tool, customer service agent with escalation logic.
Typical cost range: $350,000 — $950,000
Typical timeline: 16 — 36 weeks to production
Typical team: 4-6 engineers, 1-2 data engineers, 1 MLOps engineer, 1 product manager, 0.5 compliance analyst
Key cost drivers: Data pipeline, model development and evaluation, integration with core systems, bias testing, compliance review, change management

TIER 3 — COMPLEX AI DEPLOYMENT
Definition: Multi-agent architecture, core system integration, significant organizational change. Enterprise-scale deployment. Regulatory compliance build-out.
Examples: End-to-end claims automation, AI-powered underwriting engine, multi-agent customer service platform, real-time fraud detection with autonomous action.
Typical cost range: $1.2M — $4.5M
Typical timeline: 9 — 24 months to production
Typical team: 8-15 engineers, 3-5 data engineers, 2 MLOps engineers, 2 product managers, 1 compliance lead, 1 actuarial reviewer, program management
Key cost drivers: Core system integration, regulatory compliance (model risk management, bias audit), organizational change management, training, extended testing and validation

TIER 4 — ENTERPRISE AI PLATFORM
Definition: Platform-level AI capability serving multiple use cases and business units. Foundational infrastructure investment.
Examples: Enterprise AI governance platform, centralized ML/LLM platform, enterprise-wide agentic AI infrastructure.
Typical cost range: $5M — $25M+
Typical timeline: 18 — 48 months
Key cost drivers: Platform architecture, data infrastructure, governance tooling, organizational capability building, ongoing operations

SECTION 5: ROI THRESHOLDS AND INVESTMENT CRITERIA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Meridian applies the following financial criteria when evaluating AI investment proposals:

PAYBACK PERIOD REQUIREMENTS
- Tier 1 AI systems (Critical): Maximum 18-month payback period required for approval
- Tier 2 AI systems (High risk): Maximum 24-month payback period
- Tier 3 AI systems (Moderate risk): Maximum 36-month payback period
- Strategic/platform investments: Exception process with Board approval for longer paybacks

MINIMUM ROI THRESHOLDS
- Year 1 ROI: Not required (investment phase)
- Year 2 ROI: Minimum 15% return on total investment
- Year 3 ROI: Minimum 35% cumulative return
- 5-year NPV: Must be positive at 8% discount rate (Meridian's internal hurdle rate)

RISK-ADJUSTED ROI
All AI business cases must present risk-adjusted ROI using a standard risk multiplier based on ARM™-equivalent risk assessment:
- Green/Low risk projects: ROI multiplier 1.0 (no adjustment)
- Amber/Medium risk projects: ROI discounted by 15% for risk
- Red/High risk projects: ROI discounted by 30% for risk
- Critical risk projects: ROI discounted by 50% — additional contingency reserve required

MINIMUM BUSINESS CASE REQUIREMENTS
All AI investments above $100,000 require:
- Quantified benefit realization with named benefit owner
- Implementation cost breakdown by phase
- Total cost of ownership for 3 years including operational costs
- Risk assessment aligned to Meridian AI Risk Classification
- Change management plan with adoption milestones
- Success metrics defined before project start
- Exit/decommission plan if system does not perform

OPPORTUNITY COST FRAMEWORK
When evaluating whether to agentify a process, business cases must address:
- Cost of current manual process (FTE hours × loaded rate × volume)
- Cost of not automating relative to competitor capability
- Regulatory risk of manual process errors vs. AI errors
- Customer experience impact of status quo vs. AI-assisted process
- Strategic positioning value beyond direct financial return

SECTION 6: AVRE™ SCORE CALIBRATION FOR MERIDIAN
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

These guidelines calibrate AVRE™ scoring for Meridian's specific financial context:

ROI SCORE CALIBRATION
- Score 1-3: Payback period >36 months OR Year 3 ROI <15%
- Score 4-6: Payback 18-36 months, Year 3 ROI 15-35%
- Score 7-8: Payback 12-18 months, Year 3 ROI 35-60%
- Score 9-10: Payback <12 months, Year 3 ROI >60% OR transformational strategic value

ROE SCORE CALIBRATION (Return on Effort)
- Score 1-3: Implementation cost >$2M, timeline >18 months, significant core system changes
- Score 4-6: Implementation cost $350K-$2M, timeline 4-18 months, moderate integration
- Score 7-8: Implementation cost $85K-$350K, timeline 6-16 weeks, low disruption
- Score 9-10: Implementation cost <$85K, timeline <6 weeks, plug-and-play deployment

OPPORTUNITY COST CALIBRATION
- Score 1-3: No competitive pressure, manual process adequate, low regulatory risk
- Score 4-6: Industry peers beginning to deploy, process inefficiency noted by leadership
- Score 7-8: Top 3 competitors have deployed, process is a known bottleneck, regulatory expectation building
- Score 9-10: Regulatory requirement imminent OR competitive parity requires deployment to retain market position OR manual process is creating measurable customer or financial harm today
